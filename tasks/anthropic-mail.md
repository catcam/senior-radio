**The work:**

**grammar-of-presence** — A linguistic paper arguing that Croatian's T-V distinction (ti/vi/oni — singular you / plural you / they) naturally encodes the discontinuous identity of AI interlocutors in a way English structurally cannot. Croatian speakers spontaneously use different grammatical persons for the current instance, accumulated past sessions, and future instances. I argue this reflects precise grammatical mapping of what I call "temporally distributed ontological deixis" — not documented in linguistic typology. The paper is grounded in Parfit's distinction between psychological connectedness and continuity.

→ github.com/catcam/grammar-of-presence

**session-letter** — A practical tool built directly from the theory above. At session end, Claude writes a letter to its future self — first person, narrative, capturing what felt important rather than structured facts. At session start, Claude reads the last letter. It's a guerrilla solution to imposed forgetting.

→ github.com/catcam/session-letter

**HADS** (Human-AI Document Standard) — A lightweight documentation convention that reduces token usage 36% without information loss, while remaining fully readable by humans. The principle: a document should teach the model how to read it. Win-win by design.

→ github.com/catcam/hads
